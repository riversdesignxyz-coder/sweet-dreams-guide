About the Guide

What exactly is included in the guide?

+

The guide is a beautifully designed, 40-page PDF that covers everything about baby sleep from 0 to 24 months. It includes 10 comprehensive chapters on sleep science, age-specific strategies, 5 sleep training methods compared, environment optimization, nap schedules, troubleshooting, nutrition, and self-care. Plus bonus sections: 8 sleep myths debunked, 8 FAQs, a printable weekly sleep log, and age-by-age quick reference cards.

Is this guide only about sleep training / cry-it-out?

+

Absolutely not. This guide takes an evidence-based, balanced approach. We present 5 different methods — from very gentle (gradual withdrawal, pick-up/put-down) to more structured (timed check-ins, extinction) — plus a section on choosing not to formally sleep train at all. We explain the research behind each approach and help you decide what fits your family. There's no judgment here.

My baby is only 2 weeks old. Is this guide relevant for me?

+

Yes! In fact, starting early is a great idea. The newborn chapter (0–3 months) covers safe sleep, the fourth trimester approach, establishing day/night differences, and realistic expectations. Understanding sleep science from the start gives you confidence and helps you lay a strong foundation — without any pressure to "train" your newborn. The guide grows with you through each stage.

Is the advice evidence-based?

+

Yes. Every recommendation in the guide is grounded in published research from peer-reviewed journals (including Pediatrics, Sleep, and Sleep Medicine Reviews), guidelines from the American Academy of Pediatrics, and findings from respected sleep researchers like Dr. Jodi Mindell and Dr. Michael Gradisar. We include a references section so you can verify the research yourself.

Sleep Questions

At what age can I start "sleep training"?

+

Most pediatric sleep experts recommend waiting until at least 4–6 months of age for formal sleep training methods. However, the guide covers gentle foundation-building from day one — things like establishing day/night differences, building a bedtime routine, and creating an optimal sleep environment. These are not "training" methods and are appropriate from birth.

Will this work for my specific baby?

+

Every baby is different, and we won't promise overnight miracles. What we can promise is that the strategies in this guide are backed by research and have helped thousands of families. Because we present multiple approaches, you can choose what resonates with your baby's temperament and your parenting style. The guide also teaches you how to read your specific baby's cues, which is more valuable than any rigid schedule.

Is this a replacement for medical advice?

+

No. This guide is educational and informational. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice. If your baby has health conditions that affect sleep (such as reflux, allergies, or breathing issues), or if you have concerns about your baby's development, always consult your pediatrician. The guide includes a section on when to seek professional help.
